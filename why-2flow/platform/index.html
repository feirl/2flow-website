How it runs

What the platform does on your behalf

The warehouse management system sits at the centre of every operation. It is what makes same-day dispatch possible, what catches errors before they reach customers, and what lets the team scale through peak without slowing down.

Orders sync in minutes

Orders from your Shopify store or other sales channel sync automatically and are queued for picking within 15 minutes of being placed. No manual imports, no batched uploads.

Pick accuracy, by design

Every pick is barcode-scanned against the order. The system validates the right product, the right quantity, the right destination before anything leaves the shelf. Errors are flagged before they become mis-ships.

Expiry and rotation management

For health, food and perishable products, the platform enforces FIFO and FEFO automatically. Oldest stock ships first. Expiry dates are tracked per batch. No manual rotation required.

Built for peak

Batch scheduling groups orders for efficient picking runs. The system allocates work automatically across the team, maintaining throughput whether it is a quiet Tuesday or Black Friday weekend.

Returns handled properly

Returned items are received, inspected and logged back to stock through the same system - with full traceability. Nothing gets lost in a pile or restocked without a record.

Multi-carrier, automatic labels

Integrated with all major carriers. The platform generates shipping labels automatically and routes orders to the right carrier based on destination, weight and service level. No manual carrier selection.

Client portal

Your operation, visible from anywhere.

As a 2Flow client, you get your own secure portal. It is not a read-only dashboard that shows you what we decide to share. It gives you direct, real-time access to your stock, your orders, your inbound deliveries and your account - from any device, at any time.

Most brands tell us they check it once a day at most. The point is not that you need to watch it constantly. The point is that when you need something, it is there - and you do not need to call us to get it.

What you can access

Everything in your portal

Seven things you can do from the client portal - without picking up the phone.

Live stock levels

See exactly what you have in the warehouse right now. Units by SKU, by batch, by location. The same view the 2Flow team has - updated in real time.

Order tracking

Track every order from the moment it is placed to the moment it dispatches. See status in real time - pending, picking, packed, dispatched. No more chasing for updates.

Inbound management

Raise advance shipping notices and schedule incoming deliveries yourself. The warehouse team can plan receiving before your stock arrives, which means faster check-in times.

On-demand reports

Build and run your own inventory, order and performance reports whenever you need them. No waiting, no requests. Pull a stock report at 11pm before a board meeting the next morning.

Itemised invoices

Every charge broken down by activity: storage, pick and pack, handling, returns, shipping. You can see exactly what you are being charged for and why.

Low stock alerts

Set your own threshold per SKU. When stock drops below it, you get an automatic alert. You decide the level - the platform does the watching.

Raise and track queries

Log support queries directly through the portal and track their status. Every issue is on record, with a response thread - so nothing falls into a WhatsApp message and gets lost.

Product management

Add and update product details directly from the portal. Set preferences for stock allocation, picking method, and labelling requirements without going back and forth over email.

In practice

What this looks like day-to-day

Three things that happen differently when your fulfilment partner runs on a proper platform.

01

You stop chasing updates

Instead of emailing your 3PL to ask how many units you have left, or whether an order has gone out, you check the portal. It takes 30 seconds. Real-time data means the answer is always current - not from a spreadsheet that was updated at the end of last week.

02

You know about stock issues before your customers do

Low stock alerts fire automatically when a SKU hits your threshold. You have time to place a replenishment order before you sell out. No more discovering you have run out of your bestseller when the customer service emails start arriving.

03

Inbound stock arrives faster

When you raise an advance shipping notice before your delivery arrives, the warehouse team can plan the receiving run before the pallet comes through the door. Stock gets checked in and available to sell faster than if it arrives unannounced.
