The problem

Where workplace suppliers get stuck

Most 3PLs are built around eCommerce. That leaves workplace brands with a long list of small, costly gaps that eat margin and strain customer relationships.

Procurement portals don't behave like Shopify

Catalogue sync, punch-out, PO intake, shipping confirmation, invoicing. If your fulfilment operation can't speak Coupa, Ariba or Jaggaer, the buyer experience breaks.

Commercial paperwork is an afterthought

Missing delivery dockets, wrong PO references and late commercial invoices slow down your customer's finance team and delay your payment.

Scheduled replenishment is manual

Weekly and fortnightly site replenishment gets managed off a spreadsheet, which means missed drops, duplicated orders and customers chasing your ops team.

Common questions

Office & business fulfilment questions answered

What workplace-channel brands usually want to know before moving their fulfilment.

Does 2Flow produce commercial paperwork for B2B orders?
Yes. Commercial invoices, delivery dockets, packing lists, PO references and any buyer-specific paperwork are generated automatically alongside the dispatch and sent with the shipment.
Can 2Flow run scheduled replenishment to multiple sites?
Yes. We support scheduled replenishment across multi-site accounts, from small office deliveries up to programmes covering dozens of sites, with consolidated reporting and site-level tracking.
What picking formats are supported?
Eaches, inner cases, outer cases and pallet picks, all from the same stock. Typical workplace orders are bulk or case-pick; DTC-style singles are also supported from the same operation.
Can you support account-level pricing and PO tracking?
Yes. 2Flow handles account-based dispatch rules, PO references across deliveries, and reporting by account, site and PO so your finance and account teams can reconcile without chasing.
