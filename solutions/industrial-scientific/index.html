The challenges

Where industrial and scientific fulfilment breaks down

Traceability requirements, hazmat classifications, and B2B documentation standards make this category unforgiving. Here is where things go wrong.

Traceability requirements are stricter than general eCommerce

Industrial and scientific products are often regulated, audited, or subject to chain-of-custody requirements. A 3PL that logs at order level rather than unit level cannot support a recall, audit, or client dispute without gaps.

Dangerous goods compliance is complex and carrier-specific

Industrial supplies frequently include hazmat categories: flammables, oxidisers, corrosives, compressed gases. Each class has carrier-specific packaging, labelling, and documentation requirements. A warehouse that handles these on an ad-hoc basis is a liability.

B2B buyers have strict delivery and documentation standards

Procurement teams, quality managers, and laboratory administrators expect correct documentation on every delivery. Missing or incorrect delivery notes, certificates of conformance, or packaging compliance create rejection risk at the door.
