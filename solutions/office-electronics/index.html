The challenges

Where office products fulfilment breaks down

Running B2B, DTC, and retail from separate operations is costly. Running them through a single partner that isn't built for it is worse. Here is where things go wrong.

B2B and DTC have completely different fulfilment requirements

A B2B order might be 200 units to a single business address with compliance labels. A DTC order is 1 item to a home address with branded packaging. Running both through a single fulfilment partner is only efficient if that partner has built for it. Most haven't.

Retail replenishment requires precision labelling and timing

Retail buyers have specific compliance expectations: barcode placement, carton labelling, pallet formatting. Arriving without these means chargebacks. Arriving late means delisted. A 3PL without retail prep capability creates ongoing cost.

High SKU count makes picking accuracy critical

Office catalogues are wide. Hundreds of SKUs, many similar variants, and a business customer who notices a wrong item immediately. Picking errors in B2B don't just create a return. They damage a commercial relationship.
