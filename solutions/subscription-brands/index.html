The Fulfilment Problem

Why subscription fulfilment breaks standard 3PLs

Standard eCommerce is random order flow. Subscription fulfilment is something completely different. It runs on billing cycles, batch windows, and a level of operational precision that most warehouses are not built for.

Billing day creates a surge most warehouses are not built for

Every billing cycle, hundreds or thousands of orders land simultaneously. A 3PL built for steady-state eCommerce flow will either fall behind, cut corners, or batch-skip late-recovered payments. 2Flow plans around your billing date. The surge is expected, staffed, and dispatched on time.

A wrong box doesn't lose one order, it loses the subscriber

In standard eCommerce, a mispick is a bad day for one customer. In subscriptions, that same error is replicated across the entire batch before anyone notices. And a subscriber who gets the wrong box after being charged has far less patience than someone who ordered once. Accuracy isn't a KPI here, it's the product.

Failed payment recoveries need a defined process, not an ad-hoc fix

Up to 40% of subscriber churn comes from failed payments rather than intentional cancellations. When dunning recovers a payment after the main batch has shipped, the fulfilment partner needs a defined process for that subscriber. Without it, they fall through. With 2Flow, late-recovered orders have a clear re-fulfilment path.

How We Handle It

The systems and process behind subscription fulfilment at 2Flow

We are optimised for subscriptions. That is not a marketing line. It is reflected in how our WMS is configured, how we staff for billing cycles, and how our integrations are built.

ReCharge & Shopify integration

2Flow integrates directly with ReCharge and Shopify through Mintsoft WMS. Subscription orders sync automatically, inventory updates in real time, and billing cycle orders flow into our pick queue without manual intervention. No spreadsheets. No lag.

Billing cycle planning

We plan labour and kit assembly around your billing date, not around our standard daily throughput. Your cycle is built into our operational calendar. The batch does not compete with regular order flow for capacity or staff. It has its own window.

Batch kitting and scan-verified assembly

Every subscription box is assembled to a pick list and scan-verified before it leaves. Variable contents month to month are managed in WMS, not on paper. When your box changes, we update the kit spec in the system and the new build is enforced from the first unit.

Per-subscriber personalisation

Tiered boxes, preference-based contents, anniversary inserts, gifting rules. All managed at SKU level in the WMS. Each subscriber gets the right box based on their profile, not on who picked it that day. Personalisation at scale works because it is system-driven, not instruction-driven.

Late payment re-fulfilment process

Failed payment recoveries that arrive after the main batch has shipped get a defined re-fulfilment path, not a shrug. We maintain the kitting spec and buffer stock beyond the cycle window specifically so subscribers recovered by your dunning workflow still get their box.

Real-time inventory visibility

Live stock counts through the Mintsoft client portal. Before every billing cycle you can see exactly what is on hand, what is allocated, and what needs to be replenished. No daily download requests. No spreadsheet emailed to you on request. Just a dashboard you can check at any time.
