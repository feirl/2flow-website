The challenges

Where electronics fulfilment goes wrong

High value, strict compliance, and complex returns make electronics one of the most demanding categories to fulfil. Here is where things break.

High-value goods need verified handling at every step

Electronics aren't like books or clothing. A dropped unit, a missing accessory, or an unlisted serial number creates a dispute that costs more than the margin. Every step in the pick, pack, and dispatch process needs verification.

Returns are a major cost centre if handled badly

Electronics have high return rates and complex return reasons. Without clear inspection, testing, and grading at intake, returned stock gets written off unnecessarily or goes back to sellable in a state that triggers another return.

Dangerous goods compliance is non-negotiable

Batteries, lithium cells, and certain electronics are classified as dangerous goods. They require compliant packaging, labelling, and carrier documentation. A 3PL that isn't IATA/ADR aware will refuse your product or create compliance liability.
