The problem

International isn't just shipping further

Every border adds rules. Post-Brexit UK needs its own playbook. Every ROW country has its own documentation, duties, and rules. Errors compound.

Post-Brexit UK is a different country

Pre-2021 UK delivery was just domestic-at-distance. Now it's customs declarations, EORI numbers, VAT schemes, rejections at the border. The rules changed overnight and most Irish brands still haven't adapted.

Every ROW country has its own rules

US has its own duty threshold. Australia has GST. Canada has provincial sales tax. Documentation requirements vary by country. Prohibited items vary by country. A blanket international strategy fails in practice.

One wrong declaration = lost customer

The customer pays the duty at the door. They feel misled. They don't come back. They tell friends. International delivery failures cost more than the refund: they cost the long-term customer relationship.

Common questions

International fulfilment questions answered

Questions brands typically ask before shipping UK or further afield from Dublin.

Do you ship to the UK post-Brexit?
Yes. UK shipments from Dublin are handled end-to-end, including commercial invoices, EORI numbers, VAT treatment and customs declarations. UK customers see a smooth delivery experience, with duties and VAT handled on-brand rather than appearing as surprise charges at the door.
What countries do you ship to?
50+ countries through our international carrier network. UK, US, Canada, Australia and most of Asia are served directly. We can advise on country-specific restrictions, duty treatment, and delivery performance for any destination you're considering.
Who pays customs duties?
That depends on how you set your checkout. Two standard models. DDP (Delivered Duty Paid): you bake duties into the shipping cost and the customer sees no surprise charges. DDU (Delivered Duty Unpaid): the customer pays duties to the carrier on delivery. DDP produces better customer experience. DDU reduces the shipping line on the receipt. We advise based on product type and market.
What documentation do you generate?
Commercial invoices, customs declarations, HS code classifications, and carrier-specific documentation are generated automatically per shipment. EORI numbers, IOSS/OSS scheme details and country-specific paperwork are included where applicable. You don't create documents per shipment, we do.
How are returns from international customers handled?
Returns from the UK and rest of world are routed back to Dublin. We coordinate return shipping labels, handle customs on the return leg where applicable, and process the returns through the standard inspect-grade-restock flow. Reverse logistics is part of the service.
