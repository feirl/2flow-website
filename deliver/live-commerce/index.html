The Live Commerce Problem

Why most live selling setups let brands down

Live commerce is one of the fastest-growing channels in eCommerce. It's also one of the most logistically unforgiving. The gap between a great live event and a great customer experience is almost entirely a fulfilment problem.

Your fulfilment partner isn't built for live event spikes

A live selling event can generate a week's worth of orders in two hours. If your 3PL runs a standard daily queue, those orders land at the back. Your TikTok Live seller rating and your customers' expectations don't wait for the next morning's pick run.

Studio space and fulfilment are treated as two separate problems

Brands find a place to broadcast, then hope their 3PL can keep up. Or they broadcast from home and then hand-deliver stock to their warehouse. Neither solution scales. Having both in the same building removes the gap entirely.

Live event packaging quality gets deprioritised under volume pressure

When 500 orders land in 90 minutes, the branded tissue and inserts are the first thing to go. Your live audience just watched you present the product at its best. The unboxing experience needs to match that. Volume pressure is not an excuse for a plain brown box.
