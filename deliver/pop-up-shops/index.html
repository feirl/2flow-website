Why Own-Store Replenishment Breaks Down

Your stores deserve a fulfilment operation built for them. Not adapted from a DTC queue.

The brands that struggle with own-store replenishment are not doing anything wrong. They have the wrong operation for the job.

Own-store replenishment gets treated like any other DTC order

A box picked by your 3PL and sent with a regular parcel label to your Grafton Street store is not store replenishment. It is a workaround. Your store team is opening deliveries that are not shelf-ready, chasing tracking numbers meant for consumers, and doing the merchandising work that should have been done at the warehouse.

DTC order spikes starve your store replenishment

When your online store is at peak, most 3PLs' single fulfilment queue means your pop-up or flagship replenishment gets pushed. If your store opens on Saturday and the replenishment did not make it because Black Friday DTC volume had priority, you have lost both the weekend and your store manager's trust.

Pop-up and event logistics have zero margin for error

A pop-up opens on a specific date. The stock needs to be there, display-ready, on time. Not approximately. Not pending carrier collection. The consequences of being wrong are not a bad review. They are an empty event stand on opening day.

What We Do

A Retail Replenishment Operation That Runs on Schedule

Scheduled cycles, display-ready prep, dedicated retail lanes, and event fulfilment. Built for own stores, pop-ups, and concessions.

Scheduled Store Replenishment

Regular replenishment cycles set up per location. Weekly, fortnightly, or on-demand. Dispatch timed to your store's receiving window. Stock arrives shelf-ready without your store team doing warehouse work on the shop floor.

Dedicated Retail Lane

Your own-store replenishment runs in a separate fulfilment lane from DTC orders. A spike in online volume never delays your store replenishment. The two operations are independent.

Display-Ready Preparation

Products picked, packed, and prepared to planogram or display spec. Shelf-ready packaging, price stickering, and inner pack configuration applied before dispatch. Your store team merchandises, not unpacks.

Event & Pop-up Fulfilment

Opening day stock delivered on time, to the right location, display-ready. Ad-hoc top-up deliveries during the event. Collection or return of unsold stock managed at close.

Concession Replenishment

Department store concessions, pharmacies, and third-party retail locations supplied on their replenishment schedule. Compliance with buyer receiving requirements managed at dispatch.
