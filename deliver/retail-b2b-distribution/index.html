Why B2B Fulfilment Breaks

Retail buyers don't give second chances.

DTC fulfilment and retail distribution are fundamentally different operations. One missed delivery window, one incorrect pallet label, one ASN sent late. And you're looking at a chargeback, a delisted SKU, or a buyer relationship that takes months to rebuild.

Problem 01

Your DTC surge delays your retail orders

Most 3PLs run a single queue. When your Shopify store has a flash sale, your wholesale orders sit behind 500 DTC picks waiting to be processed. Your retail buyer doesn't care about your online volumes. They care that their order landed on a Tuesday, not a Friday.

Problem 02

Routing guide violations become chargebacks

Every major retailer. Tesco, Boots, SuperValu, Brown Thomas. Has a routing guide. Label placement, pallet configuration, delivery booking requirements, ASN timing. One deviation triggers a chargeback. At volume, non-compliance costs more than the margin you made on the order.

Problem 03

Own-store replenishment treated like just another order

Your own stores, concessions, and pop-ups have specific windows, stock configurations, and display-ready requirements. A 3PL that treats a flagship store replenishment the same as a parcel to a home address is going to let you down on the floor. Not just in the system.

How It Works

From Order Receipt to Retailer's Dock

Every B2B order follows a defined path through a dedicated retail lane. Separate from DTC, compliant from the first pick, documented from the first label.

1
Order Receipt & Routing
B2B order received. From your system, EDI, or direct portal. And routed immediately to the dedicated retail fulfilment lane, separate from DTC queue.
2
Buyer Compliance Check
Order checked against the buyer's stored routing guide: label requirements, pallet config, delivery window, documentation. Any discrepancy flagged before pick begins.
3
Pick, Pack & Retail Prep
Products picked to the buyer's spec. Shelf-ready packaging, retail labels in the correct position, inner/outer carton requirements, and any display-ready assembly applied.
4
Documentation & ASN
Packing list generated, ASN sent to the buyer's system before dispatch. Customs documentation prepared for EU and UK orders. Proof of dispatch captured.
5
Scheduled Dispatch
Order dispatched to meet the buyer's delivery window. Not the next available carrier run. Proof of delivery captured and shared. Any shortfall or variance reported to you before it reaches the buyer.
