Why Finishing Matters

Most brands lose the moment at dispatch.

You spend months on a product, a brand, a website, a campaign. Then it leaves the warehouse in a standard-issue carton with a delivery note. The last minute of the customer experience undoes the first hour.

Problem 01

Premium products in generic packaging

A €200 serum going out in the same brown box as a spare phone cable. The product is premium, the unboxing is not. For beauty, fragrance, and gifting brands, that gap shows up in repeat rate and review scores.

Problem 02

Personalisation that falls through

The customer pays for an engraving, a monogram, or a gift message at checkout, and the 3PL has no workflow to catch it. The order ships generic. The refund is the least of it. The customer tells their friends.

Problem 03

Bespoke work treated like standard pick

Branded tissue skipped. Ribbon tied wrong. Insert missing. Handwritten note forgotten. These are small details that compound. And on social media, one ruined unboxing video is seen more than a hundred clean ones.

Three Levels of Finish

Branded, Signature, or Fully Bespoke.

Not every order needs the full treatment. We run three tiers so you can match effort to price point, SKU, and campaign. Without overbuilding your cost base.

Tier 1. Branded Core
Your brand, every order.

The consistent base layer. Every shipment leaves in your branded outer, with your tissue, tape, and standard insert. The floor, not the ceiling. And the floor still beats what most DTC brands ship today.

  • Branded outer carton or mailer
  • Branded tissue and protective fill
  • Branded tape
  • Standard thank-you insert
Tier 2. Signature
Hand-finished, campaign-ready.

The tier most premium beauty, fragrance, and subscription brands run. Hand-tied ribbon, wax seals, printed cards, campaign-specific inserts. The kind of finish customers post about.

  • Hand-tied ribbon or cord
  • Wax seal or embossed sticker
  • Printed personalised card
  • Campaign or seasonal insert swap
Tier 3. Bespoke
Engraved, monogrammed, individual.

Truly one-of-one. Engraving, monogramming, handwritten notes, single-use inserts tied to the customer, name, or order. Where each box is different to the one that shipped before it.

  • On-site engraving (caps, components, keepsakes)
  • Monogramming or embossing
  • Handwritten card per order
  • Individually personalised insert or leaflet
