Why Compliance Fails

One wrong label. Three very different consequences.

Compliance errors don't look the same across channels. A retail buyer charges back. Amazon suspends a listing. A regulator requests documentation that doesn't exist. The product is the same. But the exposure is completely different for each.

Problem 01

Retail chargebacks from routing guide failures

Tesco, SuperValu, Boots, and every major retail buyer has a routing guide. Specific label positions, pallet configurations, delivery window requirements, and EDI documentation. One deviation from spec triggers a chargeback. At volume, these compound quickly and quietly.

Problem 02

Marketplace suspension from non-compliant prep

Amazon FBA shipments rejected at the fulfilment centre because FNSKU labels are misaligned, box weights are off, or poly-bag suffocation warnings are missing. Every rejected shipment delays your stock, damages your IPI score, and costs you sales velocity you can't recover.

Problem 03

Batch recall without lot traceability

A health supplement, cosmetic, or medical product gets flagged. Your regulator or retailer asks which units are affected and where they went. If your 3PL isn't tracking batch numbers and expiry dates at the unit level, the answer is "we don't know". And that's a brand crisis, not just an operational one.
